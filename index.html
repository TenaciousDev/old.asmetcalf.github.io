About Me

Software & Web.

I've always been fascinated with development and design. Writing code is really just a way of speaking to computers and applications, and that's about the coolest thing ever in my book! I've put this fascination to work over the past several years, and am now proficient in HTML5 & CSS3, Bootstrap, and the principles of responsive web design. I'm working toward proficiency in JavaScript, Node.js, and JSON.

On the programming side, I'm currently attending a Software Development Boot Camp at Eleven Fifty Academy in Indianapolis, where I'm learning in-demand Microsoft .NET technologies like C# and ASP.NET, architectural patterns like MVC and REST, and API integration. This is a fascinating topic for me, and I'm loving every minute of it!

Service & Education.

In 2016, I joined the US Army Infantry. It was something I'd always wanted to experience, and I wasn't disappointed! From Basic Combat Training through my three-and-a-half years of service, I worked with and learned from some of the best leaders the world has to offer.

As my time in service neared a close, I participated in a Microsoft Software & Systems Academy program offered to transitioning service members. There, I was certified as a Microsoft Azure Associate Administrator, and gained valuable skills in cloud computing and machine virtualization.

Having completed my time in service, I've since attended several other programs available to Disabled Veterans, such as the Cybersecurity Professional Program through Lab Four in Nashville, TN. I've also attended Indianapolis University-Purdue University of Indianapolis to study Computer Technology, and I'm currently enrolled at Ivy Tech furthering my education in Cybersecurity.

Experience.

I first discovered HTML and CSS in junior high school, back in the medieval era of Angelfire and GeoCities. From the television show fan-sites I built back then, to implementing design changes for an industry-leading web-based job board, I'm constantly looking for ways to upgrade my knowledge and gain new experience.
